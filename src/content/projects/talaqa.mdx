## Overview

**TALAQA** was my **graduation (senior) project**.

The idea started from a real problem: speech therapy exercises can be repetitive and difficult to stay consistent with — especially for young users — and progress is not always easy to track in a motivating way.

So we built TALAQA as a **therapeutic, speech-oriented game** that uses **speech recognition (Speech-to-Text)** to turn speech practice into an interactive experience. The goal was simple: make speech training more engaging, measurable, and easier to follow over time.

---

## The Problem

Traditional speech exercises can feel challenging for users to repeat daily. Motivation drops quickly, feedback can be limited, and it becomes harder to keep a steady routine — which directly affects progress.

---

## Our Solution

TALAQA transforms speech practice into a structured game flow:

- Users practice speech through guided activities.
- Speech-to-text helps validate and analyze input.
- The experience focuses on consistency, engagement, and visible progress.

---

## Outcome & Recognition

Based on what we presented in our graduation project, **TALAQA achieved 2nd place at the Innovation and Digital Entrepreneurship Conference 2024**, and it was **recognized as one of the top projects by the Software Engineering Department**.
